# Privacy Policy

**Last updated: March 2026**

Flashback is a history app built for curiosity. This policy explains what data we collect — and the short answer is: none.

---

## Data We Collect

We do not collect, store, or transmit any personal information. Flashback has no user accounts, no sign-in, and no backend server. Everything the app needs is stored locally on your device.

## Notifications

If you grant notification permission, Flashback schedules a daily local notification at 7 PM. This is handled entirely by iOS on your device. No notification data is sent to us or any third party.

## Images

Card images are loaded from Wikipedia's public servers (Wikimedia). This is a standard outbound request from your device to a public URL — no personal information is included. We do not control Wikimedia's servers. You can review their privacy policy at [foundation.wikimedia.org](https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Privacy_policy).

## Local Storage

Flashback stores two things locally on your device using iOS standard storage: which cards you have recently seen (to avoid repeats), and whether you have completed onboarding. This data never leaves your device and is deleted if you uninstall the app.

## Third Parties

Flashback does not use any third-party analytics, advertising SDKs, crash reporting tools, or tracking libraries. No data is shared with any third party.

## Children

Flashback does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

## Changes to This Policy

If this policy changes in a meaningful way — for example if analytics or ads are added in a future version — this page will be updated and the "last updated" date will reflect that.
